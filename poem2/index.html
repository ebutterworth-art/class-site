His name was Tug.



























Hers, Margo.






































Until people, seeing what was happening,


































Began calling her Cargo.




















Tug and Cargo.




















He had drive,











they said.









Lots of hair on his face




and arms. A big guy.

Commanding

voice.









She was more laid-back. A blonde.












Dreamy.











(Sweet and dreamy.)















She broke















loose, finally. Sailed away


















under her own power. Went to places











pictured in books, and some










not in any book, or even on the map.


































Places she, being a girl,































and cargo,























never dreamed of getting to.


































"Margo"

A poem by Raymond Carver
